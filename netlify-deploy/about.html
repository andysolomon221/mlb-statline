About

Baseball stats built for quick context.

MLB Statline is an independent baseball statistics tool for exploring season leaders, team context, player splits, Statcast boards, fantasy angles, news links, and batter-pitcher matchups.

The goal is to make public baseball data easier to scan and compare, especially across seasons, teams, leagues, and player matchups.

This site is not affiliated with Major League Baseball, MLB Advanced Media, Baseball Reference, Baseball Savant, or any MLB club.

How To Use This Site

Start with a question, then narrow the board.

Use Batting and Pitching to compare single seasons or year ranges, then narrow by league, team, position, or stat category.

Use Compare when you want side-by-side player comparisons across careers, selected seasons, or matching year counts.

Use Advanced and Statcast when you want rate stats, quality-of-contact context, and deeper player profiles beyond traditional box-score totals.

Use Matchups to compare a batter against a pitcher, review career head-to-head history, check pitcher handedness splits, and see how a whole roster has performed against that pitcher.

Use Splits for one-player breakdowns across seasons, and Fantasy when you want a practical board organized by fantasy needs.

Use Teams and News when you want a team-specific starting point for stats, links, communities, and current baseball headlines.

Page Guide

What each section is for.

Batting Traditional hitter leaderboards by season, custom range, league, team, position, and category.
Pitching Starter and reliever boards for wins, ERA, WHIP, strikeouts, saves, innings, and team pitching context.
Compare Side-by-side player comparisons across careers, selected seasons, or matching year counts.
Career One-player year-by-year batting or pitching logs with career totals and a Baseball Reference lookup.
Matchups Batter-vs-pitcher and roster-vs-pitcher tools for head-to-head and split context.
Splits One-player breakdowns by season range, home/away, handedness, and other split views.
Teams Club hubs with direct paths into team-filtered stats, news, communities, and official links.

Notes

How to read the numbers.

Historical range boards add each player's available seasons inside the selected window. When a player appears with a team label, the board is showing a best-current or selected context label, not always a claim that every stat in the range came with that club.

Some modern metrics, Statcast-style boards, and current-season data depend on public endpoints that can update, lag, or occasionally return incomplete results. When precision matters, use the original linked source as the final authority.
