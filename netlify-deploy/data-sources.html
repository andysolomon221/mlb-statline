Data Sources

Where the numbers come from.

Player, team, season, roster, split, and batter-vs-pitcher data are loaded from public MLB Stats API endpoints.

Statcast leaderboards are loaded from public Baseball Savant leaderboard CSV data and paired with MLB team metadata where needed.

News pages use publicly available RSS feeds and links back to original publishers. This site does not republish paid articles.

Baseball Reference links are outbound search/profile links only. This site does not copy Baseball Reference statistical tables.

Because public data endpoints can change, numbers should be treated as a baseball research aid, not an official record or betting recommendation.

What Is Calculated Here

Some boards combine public rows into new views.

Single-season boards generally reflect the selected season returned by the source endpoint. Multi-year boards aggregate available season rows inside the selected range so visitors can ask questions like who led MLB in home runs during the 1990s.

Team filters narrow results to the selected club where source data supports that context. Some labels, especially in historical or aggregated views, are used for navigation and identification rather than a full transaction-by-transaction history.

Comparison, fantasy, and matchup pages use public data to organize context; they are not projections, official awards models, or betting advice.

Corrections

Public data can move.

Current-season data may update during the day as games finish and public endpoints refresh. Historical endpoints can also change format or availability.

If you see a stat that appears wrong, send the page, player, team, season, and comparison source to mlbstatline@gmail.com.
